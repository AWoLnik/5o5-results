MHYC Saturday 10/4/99

With overcast skies, 8 to 10 knots of breeze from the SE, and a forecast of thunder storms and squalls, the day's racing was shaping up to be a challenging one.

Race 1.

Geoff and Kate Holden got away to a great start in the first race by hitting the right hand side of the course by themselves, and had a handy lead at the weather mark. Second around was Quirk/Lange followed by Porter/Holden, Dunn/Brad and Stodart/Robinson.

Quirk/Lange had good speed on the two reaches and led the fleet by the time they rounded the leeward mark. They held this lead for the next two legs and went on to win from Porter/Holden with Brian Robinson and crew third.

Race 2.

At the start of the second race there were two rain squalls out to sea travelling in the direction of the Heads. These storms were going to have a great effect on the strength and direction of the breeze later on in the race.

Brett Van Munster and Michael Bickham in 8648 sailed a smart first beat to lead around the first mark, followed by Porter/Holden, Quirk/Lange with Stodart/Robinson in fourth spot. At the wing mark Porter/Holden rounded first, then Van Munster/Bickham, followed by Quirk/Lange and Stodart/Robinson. As the fleet started down the bottom reach, the breeze swung 45 degrees to the left and increased in strength as the race squall approached. This turned the leg into a square run. Porter/Holden rounded the leeward mark just clear of four boats trying to get around the mark in second place. The fleet by now had closed right up. The next two legs were now main and jib reaches with the pressure around 18 knots. At the leeward mark the last time the breeze had dropped to 4 knots and swung around to the SW. Then halfway up the last beat the breeze had returned to the SE. Porter/Holden went on to win followed by Van Munster/Bickham and Stodart/Robinson in third place.

 MHYC Saturday 17/4/99

A perfect Autumn day with SE winds of 8 knots in the Sound area greeted the eight boats that turned up for the two races.

Race 1

The first race started at 2.00 pm with a number of boats being late for the start. Gate boat 8644 Porter/Holden made the best out of being pathfinder and led around the weather mark followed by Dunn/Shulz then Robinson/Dick. The first three placings were unchanged to the finish. Quirk/Lange in 8280 sailed well to finish fourth after being late for the start.

Race 2

Pathfinder for the second race was 8628 Robinson/Dick. Quirk/Lange took the lead halfway up the first beat with Robinson/Dick applying plenty of pressure in second position. As the leading group approached the weather mark the wind direction became quite variable. Quirk/Lange elected to go hard left, as did Porter/Holden. Robinson/Dick sailed the right hand corner while Dunn/Shulz chose the centre. Robinson/Dick were first around, closely followed by Porter/Holden, Dunn/Shulz and Quirk/Lange. At the wing mark the placings were unchanged, but a slow gybe by the leader Robinson/Dick enabled Porter/Holden to get to windward after rounding and taking the lead. The placings were unchanged at the leeward mark, with the second, third and fourth boats all rounding nose to tail together. Dunn/Shulz who were third, tacked away to clear their air and lost a lot of distance to the leaders. On the second beat Quirk/Lange sailed through Robinson/Dick to take over second position and closed in on Porter/Holden. At the weather mark the second time Porter/Holden led from Quirk/Lange and Robinson/Dick with only 25 seconds between the three boats. The placings were unchanged down the run and up the last beat to the finish.
